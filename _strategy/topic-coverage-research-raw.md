# Issues Canadians Say Matter Most (mid-2026) — Ranked by Salience

**Scope:** "Most important issue facing Canada" trackers, last 6–18 months, as of June 2026. Headline ranking anchored on the most recent **Abacus Data** wave (April 30–May 5, 2026; n=2,478; top-3-issues prompted), cross-checked against **Angus Reid Institute** (March 11–17, 2026; n=4,005), **Nanos Research** (weekly unprompted, 4-week rolling to March 20, 2026; n=1,024), **Ipsos** (Dec 8–15, 2025; n=1,502), **Leger** (Feb 27–Mar 2, 2026; n=1,627), and **Pollara/Environics**. Salience % = share naming the issue among their top three (Abacus/Angus Reid convention) unless noted "unprompted" (Nanos single-pick).

**One-line takeaway:** Affordability is in a class of its own and has been for 3+ years; the economy/jobs and healthcare form a stable second tier; Trump/U.S. relations is volatile and event-driven (faded mid-2025, partially resurged early 2026); immigration concern remains historically elevated but is now easing as policy tightens; crime spiked in late 2025 and settled back. The 2025-26 wildcard vs. typical Canadian issue rankings is **U.S./Trump**, which did not meaningfully exist as a top-tier issue before late 2024.

## The ranked list

### 1. Cost of living / affordability — **#1, ~60–66%** · trend: persistently dominant, re-accelerating in 2026
- **Salience:** Abacus **66%** (May 2026), Angus Reid **60%** (Mar 2026), the unambiguous #1 across every pollster for 3+ years. Within affordability, **groceries** is the single most-cited pressure point (~81%, Abacus). Abacus separately found **67%** say the cost of living feels "as bad as it's ever been."
- **Trend:** Was easing with disinflation, then **re-accelerating in spring 2026** — headline CPI rose to **2.8% in April 2026** (from 2.4% March), the highest in ~2 years, driven by a gasoline spike (energy +19.2% y/y; gasoline +28.6%) tied to Middle East supply disruption. Lower-income households (<$50k) hit 68% cost-of-living concern, a 3.5-year high (Angus Reid).
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan **Table 18-10-0004-01** (CPI, all-items + components) and core measures **18-10-0256-01** (CPI-trim, CPI-median); monthly *The Daily* CPI release.
  - **Bank of Canada** CPI / inflation target page (1–3% band, 2% midpoint) — the benchmark.
  - Food specifically: CPI "food purchased from stores" (**+3.8% y/y**, April 2026).
  - *DataCan already charts this* (housing/index.qmd CPI chart, table 18-10-0004-01).

### 2. The economy / economic growth — **#2, ~39–41%** (Nanos unprompted #1, ~21–23%) · trend: rising/worsening sentiment
- **Salience:** Abacus **39%** (May 2026), **41%** (April 2026). On **Nanos's unprompted** single-pick measure, "jobs/the economy" is the **#1** issue at ~**21–23%** (4-week rolling to March 2026), pulling away from everything else.
- **Trend:** **Rising as a worry.** Pollara's 31st annual Economic Outlook (Jan 2026) found **61% expect the economy to worsen** in 2026 and **59% expect employment to worsen** — among the most pessimistic readings in the survey's 31-year history. Real GDP growth has been weak and tariff-clouded.
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan GDP: **Table 36-10-0434-01** (monthly GDP by industry), **36-10-0104-01** (quarterly GDP).
  - **Bank of Canada** policy rate + Monetary Policy Report; **PBO** Economic and Fiscal Outlook.
  - OECD real GDP growth (GDPV_ANNPCT) and GDP/capita — *DataCan charts both vs. peers* (economics/index.qmd).

### 3. Healthcare — **#3, ~35–45%** · trend: stable–elevated, structurally chronic
- **Salience:** Abacus **35%** (May 2026); Angus Reid **40%** (Mar 2026) — consistently the #2 issue on Angus Reid's measure and the co-#1 issue in Atlantic Canada. Top-tier among older Canadians.
- **Trend:** **Flat at a high level** — a chronic concern, not event-driven. Access/wait-time pressure persists above pre-pandemic norms.
- **Data to follow:**
  - **CIHI Wait Times for Priority Procedures** (annual): hip/knee replacement, cataract, radiation, hip-fracture repair — % treated within benchmark (e.g., 2024: hip 68%, knee 61%, cataract 69% within benchmark; all below 2019). Median + 90th-percentile waits, national/provincial.
  - **OECD** life expectancy, avoidable mortality, physicians/nurses/beds/MRI per capita, health spending %GDP and per capita — *DataCan charts all of these vs. peers* (health/index.qmd).
  - StatCan: unmet health-care needs / access (Canadian Community Health Survey).

### 4. Donald Trump / U.S. relations / tariffs / sovereignty — **#3–4 (Abacus ~36%); but unprompted ~10% (Nanos)** · trend: VOLATILE — peaked spring 2025, faded mid-2025, partial resurge early 2026
- **Salience:** The single most volatile issue. On Abacus's prompted top-3, "Donald Trump and his administration" sits at **36%** (May 2026) — a clear top-tier issue. But on **Nanos's unprompted single-pick**, "Trump/U.S. relations" has cooled to **~10%**, less than half of jobs/economy.
- **Trend trajectory (Abacus):** ~mid-40s as a top issue in **spring 2025** (April 2025: 68% wanted leaders to *discuss* it — the dominant ballot issue during the 2025 election) → **43%** July 2025 → **34%** Sept 2025 ("Trump fades") → resurged to **35%** Jan 2026 (Greenland/Iran/Venezuela tensions) → **36%** May 2026. **Net: well off its 2025 peak but still elevated and event-sensitive.** This is the defining *new* issue of 2025-26 — it had essentially no salience before late 2024.
- **Data to follow:**
  - **StatCan** merchandise trade (**Table 12-10-0011-01**), exports to U.S. share; Canada–U.S. trade balance.
  - **Bank of Canada** tariff-scenario analysis in the MPR; **Dept. of Finance** tariff/countermeasure announcements.
  - **OECD current account** (CBGDPR) — *DataCan charts current account vs. peers*. Defence spending %GDP vs. NATO 2% (sovereignty/burden-sharing angle) — *also charted* (fiscal/index.qmd, World Bank MS.MIL.XPND.GD.ZS).

### 5. Housing affordability & accessibility — **#5, ~23–32%** (40% among 18–34) · trend: easing on prices/rents, still acute structurally
- **Salience:** Abacus **32%** (May 2026), Angus Reid **23%** (Mar 2026). **Generationally skewed:** ~**40%** of 18–34s name it (Angus Reid); Environics finds 18–39s twice as likely as 65+ to name economy/cost-of-living/housing (40% vs 16%). **9 in 10** Canadians are concerned about the state of housing (Abacus). Often folded into "affordability" rather than tracked separately, which understates it.
- **Trend:** **Cooling on the margin** as population growth reverses — average **rent down 12 consecutive months to ~$2,123**; rental vacancy up to **3.1%** (2025) from 2.2%; **New Housing Price Index −2.3% y/y** (April 2026). But price-to-income remains historically stretched (Vancouver ~11–12×, Toronto ~10×).
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan **New Housing Price Index 18-10-0205-01**; **CMHC rents 34-10-0133-01**, **housing starts 34-10-0143-01 / 34-10-0158-01**, **rental vacancy 34-10-0127-01**.
  - **CMHC** Housing Supply Report + Housing Market Outlook; **National Bank Housing Affordability Monitor** (Q1 2026).
  - OECD real house-price index + price-to-income — *DataCan charts NHPI, rent, starts, vacancy, prices-vs-incomes, plus tract/CMA dwelling-value & affordability maps* (housing/index.qmd).

### 6. Immigration / temporary residents / borders — **#6, ~17–26%** · trend: FALLING from a 2024-25 peak, but historically high
- **Salience:** Abacus **23%** (May 2026), Angus Reid **17%** (Mar 2026), Nanos top-5 unprompted. Concern **quadrupled** over 2022–2024 (≈5–6% → 21%, Angus Reid) — one of the fastest-rising issues of the cycle.
- **Trend:** **Now easing** as policy tightens and numbers fall, but attitudes have hardened: **52%** view the Temporary Foreign Worker Program negatively (Angus Reid); **65%** want provinces to control immigration levels, **72–73%** back residency requirements before social-program/health access (Leger, Feb–Mar 2026). The policy response is concrete and trackable.
- **Data to follow:**
  - **IRCC 2026–2028 Immigration Levels Plan:** PR target **380,000/yr**; new temporary-resident *arrivals* cut to **385,000** (2026) from 673,650 (2025); students 155k, workers 230k; goal of **temporary residents <5% of population by end-2027**.
  - **StatCan population estimates 17-10-0009-01** (quarterly) + components **17-10-0008-01**; **non-permanent residents 17-10-0121-01**. NPRs fell from a peak **3,149,131** (Oct 2024) to **2,676,441** (Jan 1, 2026).
  - **PBO** "Demographic Implications of the 2026–2028 Levels Plan" (Feb 26, 2026): NPR share **6.8%** (Oct 2025) → just under 5% (end-2027); population growth ~flat 2026, 0.3% 2027.
  - *DataCan charts NPRs, population total/growth/components vs. peers* (population/index.qmd).

### 7. Jobs / unemployment — **#7, ~15%** (embedded in Nanos's #1 "jobs/economy") · trend: RISING sharply, esp. youth
- **Salience:** Abacus **15%** (May 2026) as a standalone, but materially higher in reality because Nanos bundles it into the #1 "jobs/economy." Acute among the young: ~**19%** of 18–34s name lack of jobs as a top issue.
- **Trend:** **Clearly worsening.** National unemployment **6.9%** (April 2026, table 14-10-0287-01), highest in ~6 months; **youth (15–24) 14.3%**, with the number of unemployed youth **+57% in three years** (290k in 2022 → 437k in 2025) — levels unseen outside a recession (per StatCan LFS / The Hub / Fraser).
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan **Labour Force Survey, Table 14-10-0287-01** (unemployment rate, monthly, SA) + **14-10-0017-01/14-10-0020-01** (by age); monthly *The Daily* LFS release.
  - OECD unemployment + employment rate — *DataCan charts both vs. peers, plus unemployment-by-city map* (economics/index.qmd).

### 8. Crime / public safety — **#8, ~14%** · trend: spiked late 2025, settled back
- **Salience:** Abacus **14%** (May 2026). Highest among older Canadians, Ontarians, and Conservative voters; the Conservatives lead the parties on this issue 44–35.
- **Trend:** **Spiked then eased.** Rose to **20%** (Sept 12–17, 2025, from 16% two weeks earlier — one of the fastest-rising issues of 2025, around bail-reform debate), then settled to 14% by spring 2026. Underlying data is mixed/elevated vs. mid-2010s.
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan **Crime Severity Index 35-10-0026-01** and **homicide rate 35-10-0068-01** (annual, July release).
  - **CCJS** Uniform Crime Reporting Survey; police-reported crime by CMA.
  - *DataCan charts CSI + homicide vs. peers and a CSI-by-city map* (wellbeing/index.qmd).

### 9. Government deficit / spending / fiscal management — **#9, ~12% as "inequality/poverty"-adjacent; deficit-worry much higher (71%)** · trend: rising salience around the budget cycle
- **Salience:** Rarely cracks the *unprompted* top tier (sits ~8–10% on Nanos as "debt/deficit"), but **prompted concern is very high: 71% are worried about government deficits** (Leger, Feb–Mar 2026), and Canadians prefer spending restraint to tax hikes (30% cut public-service headcount / 30% cut programs / only 14% raise taxes). A Conservative-leaning frame.
- **Trend:** **Elevated around the fiscal calendar.** The **Spring Economic Update 2026** (released ~May 1, 2026) put the **2025-26 deficit at $66.9B (2.1% of GDP)**, down ~$11.5B from Budget 2025, declining to $53.2B (1.4% of GDP) by 2030-31; Canada's net-debt-to-GDP remains lowest in the G7.
- **Data to follow:**
  - **Dept. of Finance** Fiscal Reference Tables + Annual Financial Report; Budget/Economic Update documents.
  - **PBO** Economic and Fiscal Outlook + Fiscal Sustainability Report.
  - OECD general-government gross debt (GGFLQ), budget balance (NLGQ), revenue (YRGTQ), net interest (GNINTQ) — *DataCan charts all vs. peers, plus government workforce & federal spending by department/function* (fiscal/index.qmd, government/index.qmd, government/spending.qmd).

### 10. Climate change / environment — **#10, ~13%** · trend: flat-to-falling, displaced by affordability
- **Salience:** Abacus **13%** (May 2026), Angus Reid **21%** (tied with immigration, Mar 2026 — higher on the prompted list). Has been **squeezed down the agenda** by cost-of-living since 2022; carbon-pricing politics added salience but not as a top economic worry. Younger and Quebec respondents rank it higher.
- **Trend:** **Stable-to-declining** in relative rank; episodically spikes with wildfire seasons.
- **Data to follow:**
  - **Environment and Climate Change Canada** National Inventory Report (GHG emissions, Mt CO2e); StatCan **Table 38-10-0097-01** (GHG).
  - **NRCan** National Fire Database (area burned) — 2023 was a record 17.6 Mha.
  - OECD CO2 per capita + consumption-based CO2; low-carbon electricity share — *DataCan charts emissions, electricity/energy mix, and wildfire/sea-ice* (environment/index.qmd, geography/fire-ice.qmd).

### 11. Inequality / poverty — **#11, ~12%** · trend: stable, overlaps affordability
- **Salience:** Abacus **12%** (May 2026). Largely subsumed into the cost-of-living frame; named more by lower-income and younger Canadians. Food-bank usage at record highs adds salience.
- **Data to follow:**
  - StatCan **median after-tax income 11-10-0190-01**, **low income (LIM-AT) 11-10-0135-01**, **Gini** (11-10-0134-01), **food insecurity 13-10-0835-01**.
  - OECD Gini + poverty rate; real household disposable income per capita — *DataCan charts median income, Gini, poverty, LIM-AT, food insecurity, disposable income vs. peers, plus tract income map* (income/index.qmd).

### 12. National unity / Western alienation / Quebec — **outside the national top tier (~low single digits nationally) but acute regionally** · trend: rising in Alberta/Prairies
- **Salience:** Does not register in the national prompted top-10, but is highly salient regionally — Angus Reid tracked **Alberta referendum-style questions** (e.g., majorities supporting restricting temporary residents' access to provincial services; sovereignty/equalization sentiment in Alberta and Saskatchewan).
- **Data to follow:** Angus Reid Institute regional/Alberta tracking; Leger provincial-control-of-immigration series; StatCan inter-provincial migration (**17-10-0045-01**); equalization/transfer figures (Dept. of Finance). *DataCan's by-province and geography sections provide the structural backdrop.*

## 2. Housing affordability
**The statistic:** National **price-to-income ratio ≈ 8.4** (vs the 3–5 "affordable" threshold); homeownership costs **~58% of income nationally, ~82% Vancouver, ~75% Toronto**; prices **+53% (2015–2025)** vs incomes **+13%**. Named a top-3 issue by ~**32%**. (Affordability has improved for 9 straight quarters off the 2023 peak — the "is it actually getting better?" counter-debate.)
**Representative headline:** "Owning a home became more affordable in Canada but gains are slimmer" — RBC Economics

# Canadian political-economy data that goes viral: metrics, framings, and the projects that lead with them (June 2026)

The Canadian "decline-ist" data discourse on X and among data-focused projects has consolidated around **one master narrative — stagnation / "we're falling behind"** — anchored by a handful of repeatable, comparison-driven statistics. Below are the specific metrics, exactly how they are framed, who leads with them, and the underlying official sources to cite.

## 1. The #1 viral chart: GDP per capita — Canada vs the US (the "lost decade" / "stagnation nation")

This is the single most-circulated Canadian economic chart of 2024–2026. The framing is always **relative and per-capita**, never absolute GDP (because aggregate GDP grows on immigration, which is the "trick" the viral framing exposes).

**Exact framings in circulation:**
- **"Canada at 71.4% of US GDP per capita in 2024, down from 83.1% in 2014."** (The Hub DeepDive, "Why Canada's GDP per capita crisis is real," 2026-03-20.)
- **Canada grew GDP/capita 3.2% total (0.4%/yr) over 2014–2024 vs the US 20.2% (1.9%/yr) and the OECD 15.3% — "third-lowest among 38 advanced nations."**
- **"Fell below the OECD average for the first time in recorded history"** (99.5% of OECD average, 2024).
- **OECD's own forecast that Canada will be the worst-performing OECD economy for real GDP/capita growth, 2020–2060** (cited constantly by Conference Board, The Hub, Fraser).
- The "lost decade" / **"GDP per capita is below pre-pandemic levels."**
- A 2026 nuance now circulating: **"Three-quarters of the Canada–US GDP-per-capita gap is concentrated in the top 10% of earners"** (i.e., the median gap is smaller than headline).

**The "technical recession" hook (2026):** Canada posted a Q1 2026 technical recession; the viral stat is that **real GDP per capita has fallen in ~7 of the last 8 quarters** (RBC/StatCan framing).

**Underlying sources to cite:** StatCan GDP (Table 36-10-0104; income & expenditure *Daily*, 2026-05-29 for Q1 2026); StatCan analytical article **"Measuring Canada's economic performance relative to the United States"** (36-28-0001); OECD GDP-per-capita long-run projections.
- https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/36-28-0001/2026003/article/00004-eng.htm
- https://thehub.ca/2026/03/20/why-canadas-gdp-per-capita-crisis-is-real-deepdive/
- https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260529/dq260529a-eng.htm

## 2. Productivity: the "break the glass / emergency" stat

The most-quoted single sentence in Canadian econ discourse, still recirculating two years on:

**Sources:** https://www.bankofcanada.ca/2024/03/speech-carolyn-rogers-march-26-2024/ ; OECD productivity-level data (DSD_PDB); StatCan Table 36-10-0480 (labour productivity).

- Business R&D: **"1.1% of GDP vs 2.0% OECD average."**

## 4. Federal public-service headcount & cost (CanadaSpends' core franchise + PBO's recurring viral hit)

This is the **highest-engagement "gotcha" on X**, driven by **CanadaSpends** (a Build Canada-adjacent project whose charts are designed to be screenshotted) and validated by the **PBO**.

**CanadaSpends' homepage leads with (FY2024):**

- **Average compensation $136,345, +16% over 5 years**

**The PBO's viral numbers (the "official" version that journalists cite):**
- **Federal personnel spending hit $71.4B in 2024-25, up ~80% from $39.6B in 2015-16** ("cost of the bureaucracy up 80% in 10 years"). PBO projects $76.2B by 2029-30. (PBO, *Projecting Federal Personnel Expenses*, 2025; CBC 2026.)
- **Benefits/insurance costs growing 11%/yr, reaching $4.4B by 2030-31** (PBO, reported Hill Times 2026-06-02 — current).

Each topic gives the **single best authoritative primary series** (with exact table/report IDs, frequency, history length, latest value), the **conventional visualization**, and a note on **current public salience** (June 2026).

**Salience snapshot (Abacus Data, fielded Apr 30–May 5, 2026, n=2,478):** Top issues facing Canada — **cost of living 66%**, economy 39%, **Donald Trump/US 36%**, healthcare 35%, **housing 32%**, immigration 23%, jobs 15%, crime 14%, climate 13%. This ordering is the de facto priority ranking for the topics below. ([Abacus](https://abacusdata.ca/for-67-of-canadians-the-cost-of-living-feels-as-bad-as-it-ever-has/))

## 1. Recession / Economic Growth

- **Authoritative series:** Real GDP, expenditure-based — **StatCan table 36-10-0104-01** (quarterly, seasonally adjusted, chained 2017$). Companion monthly GDP-by-industry is **36-10-0434-01**. Long history (quarterly back to 1961).
- **Latest (released May 29, 2026):** Real GDP **unchanged in Q1 2026** after **−0.2% in Q4 2025**; annualized **−0.1%**, far below the Bank of Canada's 1.5% outlook. Widely reported as a **technical recession** (two consecutive quarterly declines), though StatCan/some economists note the marginal flat Q1. ([StatCan Daily](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260529/dq260529a-eng.htm); [BNN Bloomberg](https://www.bnnbloomberg.ca/business/economics/2026/05/29/canada-slips-into-technical-recession-as-economy-stalls-in-q1-statcan/))
- **Conventional viz:** Quarterly annualized real GDP growth as a **bar chart** (negative bars shaded), often with a recession-shading overlay; or a level line indexed to a base.
- **Cross-country:** OECD `DSD_EO@DF_EO` GDPV_ANNPCT (already in DataCan registry).

## 2. Deficit / Debt

- **Authoritative series (actuals):** **Annual Financial Report of the Government of Canada** (Dept. of Finance) + the **Fiscal Reference Tables** (annual). The FRT is the definitive long-run series (revenues, expenses, budget balance, federal debt/accumulated deficit, debt-to-GDP — back to 1966–67).
- **Latest actual (AFR 2024-25):** Budgetary **deficit $36.3B**; **federal debt $1,266.5B**; **federal debt-to-GDP 41.2%** (down from 42.1% in 2023-24). ([Dept. of Finance AFR 2024-25](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-finance/services/publications/annual-financial-report/2025.html))
- **Authoritative series (forward):** **Budget 2025** projections + the independent **Parliamentary Budget Officer** Economic & Fiscal Outlook. PBO projects **2025-26 deficit ≈ $68.5B (2.2% of GDP)** vs Finance Canada's Budget 2025 figure of **$78.3B (2.5%)**; PBO warns the declining-deficit fiscal anchor is unlikely to hold and finances are "unsustainable without changes." ([PBO](https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/news-releases--communiques-de-presse/pbo-releases-review-of-budget-2025-le-dpb-publie-lexamen-du-budget-de-2025); [CBC](https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/pbo-budget-deficit-forecast-1.7643267); [Globe](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/canada/article-pbo-projection-federal-deficit-economy-growth/))
- **Conventional viz:** Deficit/surplus as **% of GDP bar chart** over time (the fiscal-anchor framing); federal **debt-to-GDP line**. DataCan already charts OECD gross debt (GGFLQ) and balance (NLGQ) vs peers.

## 3. Inflation / Cost of Living

- **Authoritative series:** Consumer Price Index — **StatCan table 18-10-0004-01** (monthly, All-items + components). BoC core measures: **CPI-trim & CPI-median** (18-10-0256-01). Long history (monthly back to 1914 for the headline index).
- **Latest (April 2026, released May 19, 2026):** Headline CPI **+2.8% y/y** (up from 2.4% in March), pushed up by **gasoline/energy** (Middle East supply disruption); ex-gasoline **+2.0%**. Above the **BoC 2% target** but within the 1–3% band. ([StatCan Daily](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260519/dq260519a-eng.htm))
- **Conventional viz:** y/y inflation **bar/line** with the **BoC 1–3% target band** + 2% dashed line (exactly DataCan's existing CPI chart). For "cost of living" lived-experience, pair with cumulative price-level index since ~2020.
- **Salience:** #1 issue; **67% say cost of living feels as bad as ever**; grocery prices cited by **81%**. ([Abacus](https://abacusdata.ca/for-67-of-canadians-the-cost-of-living-feels-as-bad-as-it-ever-has/))

## 4. Housing Affordability

- **Authoritative series (multiple, each answers a different question):**
  - **Prices:** CREA **MLS® Home Price Index / national average sale price** (monthly). DataCan note: CREA is charts-only/internal. Open alternative: StatCan **New Housing Price Index 18-10-0205-01** (new builds only; understates resale).
  - **Affordability ratio:** No single official "price-to-income," but the **National Bank Housing Affordability Monitor** (mortgage-payment-as-%-of-income, quarterly) and **OECD price-to-income index** are the conventional benchmarks. National **price-to-income ≈ 8.4** (vs the 3–5 "affordable" threshold).
  - **Supply:** **CMHC housing starts** (StatCan 34-10-0143-01, monthly) and CMHC's **Housing Market Outlook / Housing Supply Report**.
- **Latest:** CREA national average **$695,412 in April 2026** (+2.2% y/y); Vancouver mortgage payment **~85% of income** (Q4 2025); 2025 housing starts **~259,000 units** (CMHC projects starts declining 2026–28). ([CMHC](https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/professionals/housing-markets-data-and-research/market-reports/housing-market/housing-market-outlook); [NBC Affordability Monitor Q1 2026](https://www.nbc.ca/content/dam/bnc/taux-analyses/analyse-eco/logement/housing-affordability.pdf); [WOWA](https://wowa.ca/reports/canada-housing-market))
- **Conventional viz:** Price-to-income or mortgage-cost-to-income **line over time**; real house-price index; housing starts vs household formation. DataCan already has most of this.
- **Salience:** #5 issue overall (32%), but **#1 or #2 for under-30s** (~60% cite housing). ([Abacus](https://abacusdata.ca/for-67-of-canadians-the-cost-of-living-feels-as-bad-as-it-ever-has/))

## 5. Immigration

- **Authoritative series:** IRCC **Immigration Levels Plan** (annual, the official targets) + StatCan **non-permanent residents 17-10-0121-01** and quarterly **population estimates 17-10-0009-01** (the realized counts). IRCC admissions data also via the Departmental Plan.
- **Latest (2026–2028 plan / 2025–2027 plan):** PR targets cut sharply — **2024 actual 464,265 → 395,000 (2025) → 380,000 (2026) → 365,000 (2027)**. **New temporary-resident arrivals** capped at **673,650 (2025) → 516,600 (2026)**, with the explicit goal of cutting NPRs to **5% of population by end-2026**. This is why Canada's **population declined for two consecutive quarters** into Q1 2026 (visible in the GDP-per-capita data). ([IRCC supplementary info](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/news/notices/supplementary-immigration-levels-2025-2027.html); [Clark Hill 2026-2028 plan](https://www.clarkhill.com/news-events/news/canada-releases-2026-2028-immigration-levels-plan/))
- **Conventional viz:** Stacked annual admissions (PR economic/family/refugee) + NPR stock as **% of population line**; population growth decomposition (DataCan already charts components of population change).
- **Salience:** 23% name it a top issue.

## 6. Healthcare Access / Wait Times

- **Authoritative series:** **CIHI — "Wait Times for Priority Procedures in Canada"** (annual): median & 90th-percentile waits and **% meeting pan-Canadian benchmarks** for hip/knee replacement, cataract, radiation therapy, hip-fracture repair (plus 5 cancer surgeries, CT/MRI, CABG). Benchmarks: surgery within 2–26 weeks depending on urgency. Emergency-department waits via **CIHI NACRS** (released April 2026).
- **Latest (CIHI 2025, 2024 data):** **68% of hip / 61% of knee** replacements within 6 months; **69% of cataract** within 112 days — all still **below pre-pandemic (2019)** levels. ([CIHI](https://www.cihi.ca/en/wait-times-for-priority-procedures-in-canada-2025))
- **Conventional viz:** % of patients treated within benchmark, by procedure and province, **bar chart** vs the benchmark line; median-wait trend. (DataCan currently uses OECD beds/physicians/nurses/MRI as proxies because CIHI has no automated feed — confirmed still true.)
- **Salience:** #4 issue (35%). Striking demand-side polling: **Angus Reid (Feb 5, 2026)** — **50% of Canadians can't access a family doctor easily / at all** (up from 40% in 2015); **~58% not confident** of timely emergency care; specialist-appointment barriers cited by **56%**. ([Angus Reid](https://angusreid.org/health-care-access-family-doctor-canada-2026/))

## 7. Productivity / GDP-per-Capita

- **Authoritative series:**
  - **Labour productivity:** StatCan **table 36-10-0206-01** (business-sector labour productivity, quarterly index; "Labour productivity, hourly compensation and unit labour cost").
  - **GDP per capita:** StatCan **36-10-0104-01** (real GDP) per **17-10-0009-01** population, or directly **36-10-0222-01**; StatCan also publishes a Canada-vs-US comparison (36-28-0001 series).
- **Latest:** Business-sector labour productivity **−0.1% in Q4 2025** (2nd decline in nine quarters), tariff-driven (goods sectors). The structural story: Canada's relative productivity has fallen **~26% vs the US since the late 1990s**; real GDP per capita has stagnated/declined since ~2015 ("lost decade"). Note the *paradox in Q1 2026*: per-capita real GDP actually **rose ~0.2–0.9%** because **population fell** while aggregate GDP was flat. ([StatCan Daily, Q4 2025](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260304/dq260304a-eng.htm); [StatCan Canada-vs-US](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/36-28-0001/2026003/article/00004-eng.htm); [Bank of Canada](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/2025/11/toward-a-virtuous-circle-for-productivity/))
- **Conventional viz:** Real GDP-per-capita **line indexed vs US/OECD peers** (the prosperity-gap chart); labour-productivity-level index over time. DataCan already charts OECD productivity (PDB_LV) and GDP/capita.

## 8. Taxes & Tax Burden

- **Authoritative series:** **OECD Revenue Statistics — total tax-to-GDP** (annual; the standard cross-country burden measure; Canada ≈ 33–34% of GDP, vs OECD avg 34.1% in 2024). Federal/national detail via the **Fiscal Reference Tables** (revenue composition) and **OECD Taxing Wages** (average tax wedge on labour).
- **Latest policy salience:** **Carney "middle-class tax cut"** — lowest federal rate **15% → 14%** effective **July 1, 2025** (full 14% rate for 2026); relief up to **$420/person/yr** (~$190 average per PBO). Also **federal fuel excise tax suspended on gasoline/diesel (April 14, 2026)** to ease costs. ([PM release](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2025/06/30/canadas-new-government-delivers-middle-class-tax); [OECD Revenue Statistics 2025](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/revenue-statistics-2025_3a264267-en/full-report/tax-revenue-trends-1965-2024_98c75833.html); [PM fuel-tax release](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2026/04/14/prime-minister-carney-suspends-federal-fuel-excise-tax-gasoline-and))
- **Conventional viz:** Total tax-to-GDP **ranked bar / line vs OECD peers** (DataCan's standard peer-comparison format); tax-wedge bar.
- **Note:** "Tax burden" is value-laden — for DataCan's non-partisan stance, the OECD tax-to-GDP comparison is the neutral primary series (already partly covered via govt revenue YRGTQ).

## 9. Crime

- **Authoritative series:** **Crime Severity Index — StatCan table 35-10-0026-01** (annual, by province/territory/CMA) and **police-reported crime rate**; **homicide 35-10-0068-01**. CSI back to 1998. Released annually each July.
- **Latest (2024 data, released July 22, 2025):** **CSI −4%** (first decline after three straight increases), driven by **non-violent CSI −6%**. **788 homicides** (8 fewer than 2023); **homicide rate 1.91 per 100,000 (−4%)**. ([StatCan Daily](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/250722/dq250722a-eng.htm))
- **Conventional viz:** CSI **line over time** (national) + **CMA ranked bar / choropleth** (DataCan already maps CSI by CMA and charts homicide). Next release: **July 2026** (2025 data).
- **Salience:** 14% name crime/public safety a top issue.

## 10. Defence Spending

- **Authoritative series:** **NATO defence-expenditure-as-%-of-GDP** (annual; NATO's standardized definition is the conventional benchmark) + **Dept. of National Defence / Budget** figures. World Bank `MS.MIL.XPND.GD.ZS` (SIPRI-sourced) is DataCan's existing cross-country series.
- **Latest:** Canada **reached the NATO 2% target in FY2025-26** (announced **March 26, 2026**) — **>$63B**, "the largest year-over-year increase in generations." At **The Hague Summit (June 2025)**, Canada committed to **5% of GDP by 2035** (3.5% core defence + 1.5% defence-related), ≈ **$150B/yr**. ([PM release](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2026/03/26/prime-minister-carney-announces-canada-has-achieved-nato-2-defence); [NATO](https://www.nato.int/en/news-and-events/articles/news/2026/03/26/nato-secretary-generals-annual-report-shows-significant-increase-in-defence-investment-from-europe-and-canada); [CBC 5% pledge](https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/canada-agrees-five-percent-gdp-defence-spending-1.7570191))
- **Conventional viz:** Defence spending **% of GDP line/bar vs NATO 2% (and now 5%) guideline** — DataCan already draws the NATO 2% benchmark line; this should be updated to flag the 2% achievement and 5%/2035 target.

## 11. Energy / Emissions

- **Authoritative series:** **ECCC National Inventory Report — "Greenhouse Gas Sources and Sinks in Canada"** (annual; total + by-sector Mt CO₂e; back to 1990). Targets are the **NDC**. Electricity mix via StatCan 25-10-0015-01 (DataCan's existing provincial series) and OWID.
- **Latest (NIR 2026, 2024 data):** Total GHG **685 Mt CO₂e (−0.3% from 2023; −10% / −78 Mt below 2005)**. Target: **40–45% below 2005 by 2030**; "with measures" 2030 projection **606–645 Mt**. ([ECCC executive summary](https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/climate-change/greenhouse-gas-emissions/sources-sinks-executive-summary-2026.html); [ECCC indicator](https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/environmental-indicators/greenhouse-gas-emissions.html))
- **Conventional viz:** Total emissions **line with the 2030 target marker** and a wedge to target; **by-sector stacked area** (oil & gas, transport, buildings); per-capita emissions vs peers (DataCan's existing OECD/OWID CO₂ charts). Worth adding the absolute Mt series + 2030 target line.

## 12. US Trade / Tariffs

- **Authoritative series:** **StatCan Canadian International Merchandise Trade — table 12-10-0011-01** (monthly; exports/imports/balance, all countries + Principal Trading Partners incl. US share). Trade-in-services and the broader **current account** (BoP) complement it. Long monthly history.
- **Latest (March 2026 data, released May 5, 2026):** **Exports to US +8.3%** (highest since March 2025); **US trade surplus widened to $7.1B**; total merchandise balance swung to a **$1.8B surplus**. But **steel exports collapsed to ~⅓ of pre-tariff value**; US tariffs of **50% on steel/aluminum/copper**, 25% on derivatives, 10% on non-CUSMA-compliant goods. Economists estimate the 2025-26 tariff cycle cut **Canadian GDP ~1.5–2%**. CUSMA review due July 2026. (April trade data releases **June 9, 2026**.) ([StatCan Daily, March 2026](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260505/dq260505a-eng.htm); [CBC tariff guide](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/trump-tariffs-canada-list-9.7105883))
- **Conventional viz:** Monthly **exports to US** (level + % of total exports) and **trade balance line**; sectoral export indices (steel/aluminum/autos) showing the tariff cliff.
- **Salience:** Trump/US is the **#3 issue (36%)**; **Leger: 82%** say 2025 US tariffs significantly hit the economy, 56% hit their household; **67%** feel Canadian security is threatened by Trump; **41%** expect relations to worsen. ([Leger](https://leger360.com/in-the-news-trump-tariffs-2025-year-review/); [Nanos via CTV](https://www.ctvnews.ca/politics/article/concerns-about-trump-and-canada-us-relations-on-the-rise-again-nanos-poll/); [Globe](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/politics/article-canada-us-risk-not-ally-poll-finds/))

## Cross-cutting notes for DataCan

- **The single most important macro story (June 2026)** is the convergence of: (a) **technical recession** (Q4 2025/Q1 2026), (b) the **US tariff shock** as primary cause, (c) a **deliberate immigration cut** that flipped population growth negative — which makes the **per-capita vs aggregate GDP divergence** a uniquely chart-worthy phenomenon right now (aggregate flat/down, per-capita up because the denominator shrank). A "population growth decomposition + GDP-per-capita vs aggregate" panel would be highly topical.
- **Series DataCan already has** that map directly: real GDP growth, govt debt/balance, CPI vs target, OECD productivity & GDP/capita, defence %GDP with NATO line, CSI by CMA + homicide, OECD/OWID CO₂, non-permanent residents.
- **Gaps worth adding given salience:** (1) **absolute GHG Mt + 2030 target line** (ECCC NIR — open data); (2) **merchandise exports-to-US share** (12-10-0011-01) as a tariff-impact tracker; (3) **CIHI wait-times % meeting benchmark** if/when a feed exists (currently no automated feed — proxies remain correct); (4) updating the **defence chart** to flag 2% achieved (Mar 2026) + 5%/2035 pledge; (5) a **PBO deficit-projection vs Budget 2025** overlay for the fiscal page.
- **Frequency/history summary:** GDP quarterly (1961–); CPI monthly (1914–); FRT annual (1966–); CSI annual (1998–); GHG annual (1990–); merchandise trade monthly (long); immigration plans annual (targets) / population quarterly; CIHI wait times annual; defence %GDP annual.

# Defining Canadian Political-Economic Issues: Late 2025–2026

**As of June 2026.** Salience ranking (Abacus Data, May 2026, "top 3 issues"): cost of living **66%** > the economy **39%** > Trump/US **36%** > healthcare **35%** > housing **32%**. ([Abacus](https://abacusdata.ca/); [Angus Reid](https://angusreid.org/health-care-access-family-doctor-canada-2026/)). 70% say the Carney government has fallen short on affordability; 67% say cost of living is "the worst they can ever remember."

A unifying macro frame ties most issues together: **Canada's per-capita economy is stalling.** Real GDP grew just **1.7% in 2025** (slowest since the 2020 contraction), was **0.0% q/q in Q1 2026** (after **−0.2% q/q in Q4 2025**), and **per-capita real GDP has fallen or been flat for ~6 consecutive quarters**. Population *declined* for a second straight quarter in Q1 2026 — a historic reversal. (StatCan [Daily, May 29 2026](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260529/dq260529a-eng.htm)).

## Tier 1 — The dominant questions (cost of living, housing, the economy, Trump/trade)

These four cluster at the top of every 2026 tracker. **Angus Reid (fielded March 11–17, 2026):** cost of living **60%** (#1, stable all year), health care **40%**, housing affordability **23%**, economy **23%**, U.S. relations **18%**, immigration **17%**. **Abacus Data (2026):** cost of living **66%**, economy **39%**, "Donald Trump and his administration" **36%**, health care **35%**, housing **32%**.
Sources: [Angus Reid, top issues](https://angusreid.org/federal-politics-canada-carney-poilievre-iran-financial-pressure-cost-of-living-inflation/) · [Abacus Data report card](https://abacusdata.ca/carney-government-report-card-2/)

### Q1. "Is the cost of living still rising?"
- **Best view:** CPI year-over-year inflation bar chart with the Bank of Canada 1–3% target band + 2% line. **This is DataCan's existing CPI chart — it's the single most important chart on the site right now.** Add a small-multiple or toggle for **food** (the most-felt component).
- **The numbers:** Headline CPI **2.4% (March 2026) → ~2.8% (April 2026, a two-year high)**, pushed up by an oil-price spike from the Middle East conflict (gasoline +~40¢/L; the BoC projects a peak near **3% in April** then a return toward 2% in early 2027). **Food** is the sorest point: **+4.8% (Jan 2026), +4.1% (Feb), +3.5% (April)**; groceries are **~27% higher than five years ago**; Dalhousie's *Canada's Food Price Report 2026* forecasts **+4% to +6%** (a family of four spending ~**$994 more**, ~$17,572/yr).
- **Source:** StatCan CPI **18-10-0004-01** (All-items + Food via **18-10-0004-03**); [Bank of Canada CPI](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/rates/price-indexes/cpi/); [Dalhousie Food Price Report 2026](https://www.dal.ca/sites/agri-food/research/canada-s-food-price-report-2026.html).
- **Frame: long time series** (the BoC target band makes "in/above range" legible). *DataCan already has this; the live-data refresh is what matters.*

### Q2. "Is housing getting less affordable?"
- **Best view:** Two complementary charts: (1) **price-to-income ratio over time** (the affordability story, DataCan's existing CREA real price-to-income line, ~5.7×→9.3× 2005–2024); (2) the **value-to-income choropleth by city** (Vancouver ~11.7×, Toronto ~10× vs Calgary ~4.6×) — DataCan already has both. Optionally add the **mortgage-payment-as-%-of-income** affordability index.
- **The numbers:** National Bank's Housing Affordability Monitor shows mortgage payment **52.3% of income in Q1 2026** — a **ninth consecutive quarterly improvement** (longest streak on record) but still far above the **40.6%** post-2000 average. **CMHC** says Canada needs **430,000–480,000 new homes/year through 2035** to restore 2019 affordability vs. a business-as-usual **~245,000–250,000** — a ~200,000/yr gap (~2 million missing homes/decade). Even a hypothetical 20% price drop leaves typical buyers locked out (Abacus). Renters are getting modest relief: CMHC reports rising vacancies and incentives; TD forecasts **~3–3.5% rent growth in 2026**, roughly half of 2024's pace.
- **Source:** [CMHC Housing Market Outlook 2026](https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/professionals/housing-markets-data-and-research/market-reports/housing-market/housing-market-outlook) + Housing Supply Report; [NBC Housing Affordability Monitor Q1 2026](https://www.nbc.ca/content/dam/bnc/taux-analyses/analyse-eco/logement/housing-affordability.pdf); StatCan housing starts **34-10-0143-01**, vacancy **34-10-0127-01**, NHPI **18-10-0205-01**; CREA HPI (charts only, per DataCan's licensing note).
- **Frame: long time series** for affordability ratios; **by-city map** for "where." (Peer comparison is weaker here — Canada's price-to-income is among the worst in the OECD, which is worth one peer-comparison panel.)

### Q3. "Are we in a recession?"
- **Best view:** Real GDP growth bar chart — quarterly (annualized) with a 0% line, plus the monthly GDP nowcast as a leading edge.
- **The numbers (timely and decisive):** Canada is in a **technical recession**. **StatCan (released May 29, 2026):** real GDP **−0.1% annualized in Q1 2026**, following a revised **−1.0% annualized in Q4 2025** — two straight declines (on a quarter-over-quarter basis growth was "essentially unchanged," which is why some economists contest the label). The driver is explicitly the **trade war**: a **fifth consecutive quarterly drop in business investment** amid U.S. tariff uncertainty, plus falling exports (vehicles), residential investment, and government spending. **But it may already be over:** StatCan's early estimate puts **April 2026 at +0.4%** as oil & gas rebounded.
- **Source:** StatCan GDP **36-10-0434** (monthly by industry) / **36-10-0104** (quarterly expenditure); [StatCan via Global News, May 29 2026](https://globalnews.ca/news/11873378/gdp-march-2026/); [CBC](https://www.cbc.ca/news/business/recession-gdp-may-2026-statscan-9.7216352).
- **Frame: long time series** (recession shading makes the call obvious). DataCan already charts real GDP growth vs OECD — add a **quarterly** Canada-only view with a 0-line for this question specifically.

### Q4. "Are we falling behind the United States?" (and the productivity question behind it)
- **Best view:** Two views: (1) **real GDP per capita, Canada vs US (and peer average), indexed** — the "living standards gap"; (2) **labour productivity vs US over time** (the cause). DataCan has GDP/capita and productivity vs OECD; **add the US as the explicit benchmark line.**
- **The numbers:** StatCan's own analysis (March 2026) finds Canada's **productivity gap with the US has widened ~26% since 2000**, the principal reason living standards diverge. Canada's **real GDP per capita (PPP) grew just ~3.2% in total over 2014–2024**; business-investment growth is forecast at only **~0.8% in 2026**. This is now a mainstream, cross-partisan concern ("comfortable decline").
- **Source:** [StatCan, "Measuring Canada's economic performance relative to the United States"](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/36-28-0001/2026003/article/00004-eng.htm); OECD productivity (`DSD_PDB`) and GDP/capita (`DSD_NAMAIN10`), already in DataCan's registry.
- **Frame: peer comparison, US-anchored** (this question is *definitionally* comparative). A long-time-series overlay of the widening gap is the strongest single image.

### Q4b. "What's happening with the trade war / Trump tariffs?"
- **Best view:** This is more news-narrative than single-indicator, but the chartable proxy is **exports to the US as a share of total exports** (diversification) and **business investment** (the channel through which tariffs bite — see Q3). A timeline annotation chart of tariff milestones overlaid on GDP is the most explanatory.
- **The numbers/context:** U.S. steel & aluminum tariffs **doubled to 50%** (June 4); Canada's countermeasures and a **"Buy Canadian" policy**; a ~$1B steel/aluminum support package; on-again/off-again talks (G7 30-day deal pledge June 16; Trump suspended talks June 27). Carney is resisting a "small deal." This sits behind the recession (Q3) and the salient "Trump" issue in Abacus (36%).
- **Source:** [CBC trade coverage](https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/carney-interview-trade-tariff-relief-u-s-9.7178960); StatCan merchandise trade **12-10-0011-01**.
- **Frame: long time series with event annotations** (export share over decades shows how exposed Canada is); supplement with the investment channel.

## Tier 2 — Public finances, government size, taxes

### Q5. "How big is the federal deficit / is the budget balanced?"
- **Best view:** **Budget balance as % of GDP over time** (DataCan has this vs OECD) plus a **deficit dollar bar with the forward projection track**.
- **The numbers:** **Budget 2025 (Champagne, tabled Nov 4, 2025, "Building Canada Strong")** projects a **$78.3B deficit for 2025-26 (2.5% of GDP)**, declining to **$56.6B by 2029-30 (1.5%)**. New fiscal anchors: balance **day-to-day operating** spending by 2028-29 + a declining deficit-to-GDP ratio, paired with a **$60B "Comprehensive Expenditure Review"** savings target. **The PBO is skeptical:** it pegs the deficit higher and warns there's only a **~7.5% chance** the declining-deficit anchor holds every year through 2029-30. Note: the average annual deficit under Budget 2025 is **more than double** the 2024 Fall Economic Statement projection.
- **Source:** [Budget 2025 Annex 1](https://budget.canada.ca/2025/report-rapport/anx1-en.html); Dept. of Finance **Fiscal Reference Tables**; [PBO, *Budget 2025: Issues for Parliamentarians* (Nov 14, 2025)](https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2526-017-S--budget-2025-issues-parliamentarians--budget-2025-enjeux-parlementaires); StatCan general-government balance **36-10-0477-01** (DataCan already uses this).
- **Frame: long time series** for the balance; **add a forward projection segment** (the Budget-vs-PBO divergence is the live story). A by-government/by-era overlay (deficit by PM era) is a strong secondary.

### Q6. "How big is the national debt — and is it sustainable?"
- **Best view:** **Net debt-to-GDP, Canada vs G7/OECD** (DataCan has gross debt vs OECD; **net debt is the fairer G7 frame**) + **debt-service (interest) costs as % of GDP / % of revenue** over time.
- **The numbers:** Canada still has the **lowest net debt-to-GDP in the G7** and remains **AAA-rated** (Moody's/S&P/DBRS; AA+ Fitch). Gross general-government debt ~**69% of GDP (Mar 2025)**. **Public debt charges $58.7B in 2026-27 (1.7% of GDP)**, projected to rise to ~2.1% by 2030-31 — still below the 40-year average of ~3.2%, but interest is now **~9.7% of total government expenses** (up from 9.07% the year before). Critics (Fraser) flag combined federal-provincial debt surpassing **$2.4T in 2025-26**.
- **Source:** [Budget 2026 Spring Update, Annex 3 (Debt Management)](https://budget.canada.ca/update-miseajour/2026/report-rapport/anx3-en.html); StatCan **38-10-0237-01** (gen-gov financial indicators); OECD Economic Outlook (`DSD_EO`, GGFLQ / GNINTQ — already in registry).
- **Frame: peer comparison** (the G7 ranking is the headline and is *favourable* to Canada — a good non-partisan counterweight to deficit alarm); **long time series** for interest costs.

### Q7. "Is government bigger than it used to be?"
- **Best view:** **Public-sector employment as a share of total employment over time** + **federal program spending as % of GDP**. DataCan's Government section already has employment-by-level and spending-%GDP-since-1961 — this question is well-served.
- **The numbers:** **21.8% of employed Canadians worked in the public sector in January 2026** (4.597M), up from **~19.6% in early 2020** — the highest since the early-1990s peak of **22.3% (1993)**. The **federal public service grew ~111,000 positions over the past decade**; the **Carney government has proposed cutting ~40,000** (≈one-third of that growth) and reducing FTEs via the expenditure review. 2026-27 Main Estimates: **$502.8B** total budgetary spending (transfers ≈59.8%).
- **Source:** StatCan LFS public-sector employment **14-10-0027-01**; employment by level **36-10-0489-01**; [PBO, *Projecting Federal Personnel Expenses*](https://www.pbo-dpb.ca/en/publications/RP-2526-008-S--projecting-federal-personnel-expenses--projection-depenses-federales-consacrees-personnel); [The Hub on the 21.8% figure](https://thehub.ca/2026/02/20/more-than-one-in-five-canadians-now-works-for-government-and-the-share-is-rising/).
- **Frame: long time series** (the "back toward early-1990s levels" framing is the story); secondary **by-era** (which governments grew/shrank the share).

### Q8. "Am I paying more tax than before?"
- **Best view:** **Total tax revenue as % of GDP over time** (the macro answer) is the cleanest reproducible series; supplement with a **statutory-rate timeline** annotation for recent changes. (A true "average household tax bill" series is harder and tends to come from advocacy shops — keep to StatCan macro data for non-partisan footing.)
- **The numbers / recent changes:** The Carney government **cut the lowest marginal rate from 15% to 14%** (effective **July 1, 2025**; ~$420/person, up to ~$840 for two-income families; ~$27B over five years), **eliminated GST for first-time homebuyers** on new homes up to $1M (Bill C-4, saving up to ~$50,000), and **suspended the federal fuel excise tax** on gasoline/diesel (April 14, 2026) in response to the oil spike. - **Source:** StatCan government revenue **36-10-0477-01** (revenue line, already in registry as `govt revenue YRGTQ` via OECD too); [Dept. of Finance, "Bring down costs" motion](https://www.canada.ca/en/department-finance/news/2025/05/government-tables-a-motion-to-bring-down-costs-for-canadians.html); [PMO, GST for first-time buyers](https://www.pm.gc.ca/en/news/news-releases/2025/03/20/prime-minister-mark-carney-will-eliminate-gst-for-first-time-homebuyers).
